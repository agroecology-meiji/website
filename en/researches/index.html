Research

We view agricultural fields not only as places for crop production, but also as ecosystems shaped by diverse biological interactions. Building on ecological and data-driven studies of mycorrhizal fungi and microbial communities, we extend these approaches to understand relationships among crops, soils, and microbes, and to explore biological functions that support sustainable agriculture.

Ecology for Agriculture

Concept

Bringing ecological knowledge to agriculture

We focus not only on which organisms are present, but also on when, where, and how they function. Through field surveys, cultivation experiments, DNA sequencing, omics analysis, and data science, we study interactions among plants, fungi, bacteria, insects, and their environments.

Topics

Research themes

Symbiosis

Understanding plant–microbe symbioses

Rhizosphere microbes, including mycorrhizal fungi, are deeply involved in plant nutrient acquisition and stress responses. We build on our studies of mycorrhizal symbiosis to understand and utilize symbiotic functions in agricultural fields and crop rhizospheres.

  • Mycorrhizal and rhizosphere microbial communities
  • Plant nutrient acquisition and stress responses
  • Crop–microbe interactions
Agroecology

Applying biodiversity and interactions to agriculture

Agricultural fields include crops as well as microbes, insects, weeds, and surrounding vegetation. By understanding biological interactions, we explore ecosystem designs that support productivity, stability, and resilience.

  • Biological interaction networks
  • Diversity and ecosystem stability
  • Ecosystem functions in and around fields
Phenology

Tracking ecosystem responses to environmental change

Temperature, moisture, and soil conditions alter the activities of plants and microbes across seasons and years. Using long-term and time-series perspectives, we investigate conditions that make agroecosystems more robust to environmental change.

  • Phenology and long-term monitoring
  • Environmental DNA and amplicon sequencing
  • Links to temperature, moisture, and soil conditions
Data-driven

Visualizing hidden ecosystems through data

We integrate DNA, omics, and environmental data to quantitatively evaluate microbial communities and plant responses. Data analysis and visualization help translate hidden biological functions into interpretable information.

  • R / Python-based data analysis
  • Multivariate analysis and predictive modeling
  • Biosensing with omics data
